whatHappens:
  - "The TPA issues the Approval Letter to the hospital, typically within 4 hours for emergencies and 12 hours for planned admissions."
  - "The Approval Letter states the sanctioned amount, any conditions or exclusions, the validity period, and whether a co-pay applies."
  - "The hospital can only begin cashless treatment once this letter is in hand — without it, treatment may be billed to you directly."
  - "Ask the insurance desk for a copy of the letter for your own records; hospitals do not always volunteer this."
  - "Check whether the sanctioned amount covers the estimated bill — if not, an Enhancement Request (step A.5) will be needed before your stay ends."

The Approval Letter is the cashless path's most important milestone. Verify the sanctioned amount against the estimated bill the moment you receive a copy — catching a shortfall early gives the hospital time to file an enhancement before costs escalate.
