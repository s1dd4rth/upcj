  - "DOC-010 — Discharge Summary: issued by the treating doctor on hospital letterhead. Must be signed by the treating doctor (not a junior resident). Contains the final diagnosis, all procedures performed, medications administered, and follow-up instructions."
  - "DOC-011 — Final Hospital Bill (Itemised): issued by the billing department. Must be itemised — room charges, doctor fees, anaesthesia, investigations, pharmacy, and consumables listed separately. If the hospital offers only a summary total, ask explicitly for the itemised breakup."

Hospitals do not automatically hand over all records at discharge. You must ask each department individually. It is extremely difficult to retrieve originals after you have left the hospital, so do not proceed to discharge without all three documents in hand.
