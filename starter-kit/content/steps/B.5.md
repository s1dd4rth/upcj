whatHappens:
  - "The TPA reviews the submitted claim and may raise a deficiency: a request for missing signatures, additional documents, clarifications on billed items, or proof of payment."
  - "Deficiency letters are sent to you directly — unlike the cashless path where the hospital manages queries. You are responsible for gathering and submitting the requested items."
  - "Respond within the TPA's stated SLA, typically 15 days per the governance model. If you do not respond in time, the TPA may process the claim with the documents already on file, which can result in a lower settlement or rejection."
  - "Send responses by tracked courier. Include a cover letter listing every document enclosed, and reference the query or deficiency number on the letter."
  - "If the TPA is unresponsive, raises unreasonable queries, or issues an unjust rejection: escalate to IRDAI Bima Bharosa (call 155255) or write to the Insurance Ombudsman for your region."

Keep a record of every query, your response, and the date sent. If the TPA rejects your claim or fails to acknowledge your response within 48 hours, you have grounds for a formal grievance. The escalation path is: TPA CRO → insurer grievance cell → IRDAI (155255) → Insurance Ombudsman.
