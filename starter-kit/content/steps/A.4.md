whatHappens:
  - "The hospital treats you under the terms of the Approval Letter; covered room, doctor, and procedure charges are billed directly to the insurer."
  - "Consumables — gloves, syringes, cotton, bandages — are typically not covered by insurance and must be paid by you. Ask the nursing station which consumable charges apply."
  - "Personal items — food, laundry, attendant charges, phone hire — are not covered. Keep receipts for everything you pay for; these are not claimable but receipts help dispute erroneous charges later."
  - "Collect the daily treatment chart if you can — photograph the bedside chart each morning. If a bill is disputed at discharge, this is your evidence."
  - "If your treatment extends beyond the approval validity period or costs rise above the sanctioned amount, the hospital must file an Enhancement Request — see step A.5."
sayThis: "Can you tell me which charges on today's bill are not covered by insurance so I can pay those separately?"

Your main task during the stay is to keep records: daily treatment charts, receipts for out-of-pocket payments, and a note of every person you speak to at the insurance desk. This makes discharge much smoother.
