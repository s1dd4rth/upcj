title: "Query / Deficiency Loop"
phase: 2
path: "cashless"
lede: "The TPA may raise queries asking for more medical information before approving the pre-auth. The hospital must respond within 24 hours — but you need to actively follow up to keep this moving."
whatHappens:
  - "The TPA reviews the pre-auth form and may raise a query — called a deficiency — asking for additional reports, doctor notes, or clinical justification."
  - "The query goes to the hospital, not directly to you. The hospital and treating doctor must respond within 24 hours per the governance SLA."
  - "If the hospital does not respond in time, the TPA will process the pre-auth with the documents already on file, which can result in a lower sanctioned amount or a rejection."
  - "Your role is to be the nag: ask the insurance desk at least twice a day whether the TPA has raised any queries and whether those queries have been answered."
  - "If the pre-auth lapses unanswered, the cashless path may close. Act immediately — do not assume the hospital is handling it."
sayThis: "Has the TPA raised any queries on our pre-auth? Have those been answered yet?"
nextStepId: "A.3"
---

There may be no query at all — many pre-auths are approved without a deficiency round. But if one is raised, the 24-hour response window closes fast. Check with the insurance desk proactively rather than waiting to be told.
