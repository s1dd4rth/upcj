title: "IPD Treatment (Reimbursement)"
phase: 2
path: "reimbursement"
lede: "You are receiving treatment and paying for it yourself. Your task during this phase is to preserve every bill and document so the claim file is complete."
whatHappens:
  - "Treatment proceeds as normal. The clinical care is the same whether your path is cashless or reimbursement — the difference is who pays at each stage."
  - "You pay out of pocket for the complete treatment: room charges, doctor and surgeon fees, anaesthesia, investigations, pharmacy, consumables."
  - "Keep every bill, prescription, and treatment record as you go. Do not wait until discharge to collect these — hospital departments do not always retain copies."
  - "Note which expenses are covered by your policy and which are personal. Non-medical expenses (food, attendant charges, telephone) are typically excluded."
  - "Photograph every document with your phone as a backup. If an original is lost before submission, a clear photo may help — though the TPA will still require the original."
sayThis: "Can I have a copy of today's treatment and the corresponding pharmacy bill, stamped by the pharmacy?"
nextStepId: "B.3"

The reimbursement path places the document burden on you rather than the hospital. Stay organised from day one. A complete file assembled during treatment is far easier to manage than one assembled from memory at discharge.
